Zero
Conditional

If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

if + present present simple
The idea

What is the zero conditional?

Used for things that are always true — scientific facts and general truths. It's not about a specific moment, but what always happens.

Examples

If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.

Plants die if they don't get enough water.

The structure

if + present simple

→ present simple

Both clauses, present simple. Always.

💡 Handy fact

If and when mean the same thing here

If you freeze water, it becomes ice. = When you freeze water, it becomes ice.
When I'm tired, I make mistakes. = If I'm tired, I make mistakes.
Don't confuse them

Zero vs first conditional

Zero — always true

If you touch fire, it burns you.

First — specific future

If you touch that fire, you will burn yourself.

In real sentences

See it in action

If it rains, the ground gets wet.
If you mix red and blue, you get purple.
Plants die if they don't get enough water.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

If you will heat water, it boils.

If you heat water, it boils.

If it rains, the ground will get wet.

If it rains, the ground gets wet.

Plants dies if they don't get water.

Plants die if they don't get water.

If you mixes red and blue, you get purple.

If you mix red and blue, you get purple.
