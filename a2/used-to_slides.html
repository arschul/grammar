Used To

I used to live in London. (but not anymore)

past habit no longer true
The idea

Past habits that no longer exist

Used to describes something that happened repeatedly in the past or a state that existed before — but no longer happens or is true now.

Examples

I used to live in London. (I don't now)

She used to play the piano every day. (she doesn't anymore)

The structure

subject + used to + base verb

Always past — no present form.

The forms

Positive, negative and question

Positive

I used to walk to school.
She used to be shy.

Negative

He didn't use to eat vegetables.
We didn't use to have a car.

Question

Did you use to walk to school?
Did she use to live here?

Note: used to (positive) vs use to (after did/didn't)

💡 Important

Used to only lives in the past. There is no present form.

Past habit ✓

I used to walk to school.

Present habit → use present simple

I usually walk to school. ✓
I use to walk to school. ✗

In real sentences

See it in action

They used to be best friends. (they aren't now)
He didn't use to eat vegetables, but now he loves them.
Did you use to walk to school when you were young?
We didn't use to have a car — we went everywhere by bus.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I use to play football.

I used to play football.

I didn't used to like it.

I didn't use to like it.

I use to go to the gym. (present habit)

I usually go to the gym.

Did she used to live here?

Did she use to live here?
