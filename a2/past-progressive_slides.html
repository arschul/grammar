A2 · English Grammar

Past
Progressive

I was cooking when the phone rang.

was / were verb + -ing
The idea

An action in progress in the past

Something was already happening at a point in the past. Structure: was/were + verb‑ing.

Examples

I was watching TV at eight o'clock.

They were playing outside all afternoon.

The structure

was/were + verb‑ing

Negative: wasn't / weren't + verb‑ing

Was or were?

Choose the right form

I / he / she / it
was
She was reading a magazine.
you / we / they
were
We were waiting for the bus.

Negative: I wasn't listening. · They weren't sleeping.

Key use

Background action + interruption

I was cooking dinner when the phone rang.

cooking = in progress · rang = shorter interruption

While we were watching the film, the power went out.

while = signals the longer background action

She was walking to school when it started to rain.

when = signals the interrupting action

Spelling

Making the -ing form

Most verbs → add -ing

watch → watching
play → playing
read → reading

Silent -e → drop e, add -ing

write → writing
make → making
drive → driving

Short vowel + consonant → double

run → running
sit → sitting
swim → swimming

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

The children was playing.

The children were playing.

I were walking home.

I was walking home.

She was writeing a letter.

She was writing a letter.

He was runing across the street.

He was running across the street.
