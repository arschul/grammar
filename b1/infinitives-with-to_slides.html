The structure

Four uses, one form: to + base verb

a
to express purpose — "What for?"
b
verb + direct object + infinitive
c
verb + infinitive (no object)
d
adjective + infinitive
a · Purpose

Why someone does something

She went to the library to study. (Why? To study.)
He called me to ask for help.
I save money to travel every summer.
b · Verb + object + infinitive

A person comes between

advisetellwarn persuadeaskencourage inviteremindteach
She told me to be quiet.
My teacher encouraged me to apply for the scholarship.
c · Verb + infinitive

No object in between

agreewantprefer decidehopeneed planrefuseoffer
I want to see that film.
He refused to answer the question.
d · Adjective + infinitive

After certain adjectives

It is easy to understand.
She was happy to hear the news.
Are you ready to start?
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She told me be quiet.

She told me to be quiet.

He went to the shop for buy bread.

He went to the shop to buy bread.

I want go home.

I want to go home.

It's important study every day.

It's important to study every day.
