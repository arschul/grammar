The structure

have / has + past participle

Positive

I have visited Paris.
She has eaten sushi.

Negative

I haven't been to Japan.
He hasn't tried surfing.

Question

Have you ever been to London?
Has she ever tried skiing?

Ever and never

Life experiences

Have you ever ridden a motorbike? (any time in your life?)
Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
She has never tried sushi. (at no time)
💡 Already, yet, just

Three useful adverbs

I have already finished. (sooner than expected)
Have you finished yet? / She hasn't arrived yet. (by now?)
I've just called her. (moments ago)
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I have went to Paris.

I have gone to Paris.

She has saw that film.

She has seen that film.

He have eaten sushi.

He has eaten sushi.

I have visited Paris last year.

I visited Paris last year. (specific time → simple past)
