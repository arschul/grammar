Participial
Adjectives

The film was boring. I was bored.

-ing -ed
The idea

Two adjectives, one verb

Many adjectives come in pairs from the same verb. The ending tells you who or what we're describing.

Examples

The film was boring. → The film caused boredom.

I was bored. → I felt boredom.

The rule

-ing → the cause

-ed → the feeling

Same verb, opposite focus.

The choice

Which ending do I use?

-ing
Describes the thing or situation that causes the feeling
The news was shocking.
It was an exhausting journey.
-ed
Describes how a person feels
We were shocked by the news.
She felt completely exhausted.

-ing (cause)

-ed (feeling)

She was embarrassed when she walked into the wrong classroom.

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I was boring at the party.

I was bored at the party.

The documentary was very interested.

The documentary was very interesting.

She gave an excited speech.

She gave an exciting speech.

It was an exhausted journey.

It was an exhausting journey.
