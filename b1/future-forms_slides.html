The four forms

Four ways to talk about the future

will
predictions, spontaneous decisions, offers
going to
intentions/plans, predictions based on evidence
present progressive
fixed arrangements with another person
simple present
scheduled events — timetables, programmes
Will

Predictions & spontaneous decisions

I think it will rain tomorrow. (prediction)
She won't make that mistake again. (negative prediction)
I'll have the pasta, please. (decided right now)
Going To

Plans & predictions with evidence

I'm going to study law. (intention — already decided)
It's not going to rain. (look at the clear sky — evidence now)
Are you going to travel this summer?
Arrangements & schedules

Two more future forms

He's seeing her tomorrow. (fixed plan with someone — present progressive)
School starts at 9 a.m. (timetable — simple present)
The train doesn't leave until tomorrow morning.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I going to study tonight.

I'm going to study tonight.

The film wills start at 8.

The film will start at 8. (no -s)

I will to call you later.

I will call you later. (no "to")

She going to travel next month.

She's going to travel next month.
