present & past 5 tenses
The overview

Five tenses — one slide

Simple present
work / works
habits, facts, routines
Present progressive
am/is/are + -ing
actions in progress now; temporary situations
Simple past
worked / went
completed actions at a specific past time
Past progressive
was/were + -ing
ongoing past action; interrupted action
Present perfect
have/has + past participle
experience; recent event; unfinished period
Use 1

Present simple vs progressive

Simple present

She works at a hospital.

→ general truth / permanent job

I drink coffee every morning.

→ regular habit

Present progressive

She is working late tonight.

→ temporary / happening now

I am drinking coffee right now.

→ action at this moment

Use 2

Past simple vs progressive

Simple past

He left the office at six.

→ completed action, specific time

The phone rang.

→ sudden interrupting event

Past progressive

I was cooking when she called.

→ ongoing background action

It was raining all afternoon.

→ extended past action

Use 3

Present perfect vs simple past

Present perfect

I have seen that film.

→ life experience, time not given

She has broken her leg.

→ result still affects now

Simple past

I saw that film last Friday.

→ specific past time stated

She broke her leg two weeks ago.

→ finished, time reference given

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I was knowing the answer.

I knew the answer. ✓ (stative verb — no -ing)

Did you ever try sushi?

Have you ever tried sushi? ✓ (experience → present perfect)

She has called me last night.

She called me last night. ✓ (specific time → simple past)

He works here at the moment.

He is working here at the moment. ✓ (temporary = progressive)

Recap

Remember this

Habit / fact
→ simple present
Right now / temporary
→ present progressive
Specific past time
→ simple past
Experience / unfinished
→ present perfect

If you can say when, use the simple past; if the time is unknown or ongoing, reach for the present perfect.
