So / Such

The film was so boring. It was such a boring film.

so + adj/adv such (a/an) + noun
The key difference

What comes after each one?

so
adjective or adverb — no noun

The film was so boring.
She speaks so quickly.

such
(a/an) + adjective + noun

It was such a boring film.
They are such kind people.

Using SO

Before adjectives and adverbs

The film was so boring that I fell asleep. (adjective)
She speaks so quickly that I can't understand her. (adverb)
He ran so fast that he broke the record. (adverb)
Using SUCH

Before noun phrases

It was such a beautiful day that we went to the park. (singular countable)
They are such kind people that everyone loves them. (plural)
It was such bad weather that we stayed inside. (uncountable)
💡 Quick test

Can you replace it with very? → use so.
Does it go before a noun? → use such.

She is so tall. (very tall ✓ — no noun)
She is such a tall woman. (before noun)
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

It was so a good film.

It was such a good film.

She is such beautiful.

She is so beautiful.

He spoke such quickly.

He spoke so quickly.

It was such good weather that we stayed out.

It was such good weather that we stayed out. ✓ (no article — uncountable)
