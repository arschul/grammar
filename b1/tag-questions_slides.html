Tag
Questions

You are a teacher, aren't you? She can swim, can't she?

positive → negative tag negative → positive tag
The rule

Opposite polarity — always

Positive → Negative tag

You are a teacher, aren't you?
She works here, doesn't she?

Negative → Positive tag

They aren't ready, are they?
He doesn't drive, does he?

With to be

Use the same form of be

You are a teacher, aren't you?
She is at home, isn't she?
They aren't ready, are they?
It isn't cold today, is it?
With do / does

Use do or does — never the main verb

You like coffee, don't you?
She works here, doesn't she?
They don't eat meat, do they?
He doesn't drive, does he?
With modals

Echo the same modal

She can swim, can't she?
You will come, won't you?
He can't drive, can he?
They won't be late, will they?
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She is a doctor, isn't it?

She is a doctor, isn't she?

You like pizza, don't he?

You like pizza, don't you?

He can swim, can't they?

He can swim, can't he?

She works here, doesn't it?

She works here, doesn't she?

Remember this

Positive → negative tag
are → aren't · do → don't
Negative → positive tag
isn't → is · doesn't → does
Tag pronoun
must match the subject
Modals
can → can't · will → won't
