Second
Conditional

If I had more money, I would travel the world.

if + past simple would + base verb
The idea

Imaginary situations & results

We use it for situations that are not real — fantasies, hypothetical questions, unlikely scenarios.

Examples

If I had more money, I would travel the world. (I don't)

If he studied more, he could pass his exams.

The structure

if + past simple

→ would/could/might + base verb

Don't confuse them

First vs second conditional

First — real / likely

If it rains, I will stay at home.
(could actually happen)

Second — imaginary / unlikely

If it rained all year, I would move countries.
(not really going to happen)

💡 If I were you…

Use were for all subjects in formal English.

If I were you, I would apologise. (recommended)

If she were taller, she could be a model.

In real sentences

See it in action

If I were you, I would apologise immediately.
She would be happier if she lived closer to her family.
What would you do if you lost your job?
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

If I would have money, I would travel.

If I had money, I would travel.

If she would study, she could pass.

If she studied, she could pass.

I would to travel the world.

I would travel the world. (no "to")

What would you do if you will lose your job?

What would you do if you lost your job?
