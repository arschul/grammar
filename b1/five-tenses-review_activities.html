Review of Tenses

Five Tenses at a Glance

These five tenses each have a distinct use. The key is choosing the right one for the time frame and the nature of the action.

TenseFormKey use
Simple presentwork / workshabits, facts, routines
Present progressiveam/is/are + -ingactions in progress now or around now
Simple pastworked / wentcompleted actions at a specific past time
Past progressivewas/were + -ingaction in progress at a past moment; interrupted action
Present perfecthave/has + past participlepast experience with present relevance; recent events

Simple Present vs Present Progressive

The simple present describes what is generally true or regularly happens. The present progressive describes what is happening at this moment or temporarily.

Simple present: She works at a hospital. (her job — always true)
Present progressive: She is working late tonight. (just this evening)
I drink coffee every morning. → habit
I am drinking coffee right now. → happening now
They live in Madrid. → permanent fact
They are living in Madrid for the summer. → temporary

Simple Past vs Past Progressive

The simple past describes a completed past action. The past progressive describes an action that was in progress at a past moment — often interrupted by a simple past action.

Simple past: She called at 8pm.
Past progressive: I was cooking when she called.
He left the office at six. → completed action
It was raining when we arrived. → background + event
She was reading all evening. → extended past action

Present Perfect vs Simple Past

Use the present perfect when the exact time is unknown or irrelevant, or when the result matters now. Use the simple past when you give a specific time.

Present perfect: I have seen that film. (experience; when = not important)
Simple past: I saw that film last Friday. (specific time given)
She has broken her leg. → result affects now
She broke her leg two weeks ago. → specific past time
Have you ever tried sushi? → life experience
⚠ Common mistakes:
✗ I am working here since 2019. → ✓ I have been working here since 2019.
✗ While I cooked, the phone rang. → ✓ While I was cooking, the phone rang. (ongoing action = progressive)
✗ Did you ever visit Rome? → ✓ Have you ever visited Rome? (life experience = present perfect)
