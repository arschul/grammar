Defining
Relative Clauses

The clause that tells us which one we mean.

who which where when
The idea

What is a defining relative clause?

It gives essential information about a noun. Without it, we don't know which person, thing, place or time we mean.

Examples

She's the teacher who helped me most.

This is the city where I was born.

The structure

noun + relative pronoun + rest of clause

No commas around the clause.

The choice

Which pronoun do I use?

For a person
whothat
The woman who called left a message.
For a thing
whichthat
The phone which does everything is expensive.
For a place
where
The café where we met is closed now.
For a time
when
There are times when I want to switch off.
💡 Handy shortcut

You can use that instead of who or which.

The inventors that are creating new things…

The device that does everything…

In real sentences

See them in action

person

There are inventors who are creating amusing things.

thing

The invention that is most worrying is the 3-D printer.

place

I'm on a street where young people hang out.

time

If there's a time when people make their own products, it's now.

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She's the person which called.

She's the person who called.

That's the place who I grew up.

That's the place where I grew up.

I remember the day which we met.

I remember the day when we met.

She's the woman who she helped me.

She's the woman who helped me.
