B1 · English Grammar

Simple Present
Passive

English is spoken all over the world.

am / is / are past participle
Active vs passive

The receiver becomes the subject

People speak English all over the world.
→
English is spoken all over the world.
The company makes the product in Brazil.
→
The product is made in Brazil.
They clean the office every day.
→
The office is cleaned every day.
The structure

am / is / are + past participle

Positive

English is spoken here.
The rules are explained at the start.

Negative

The office isn't cleaned on Sundays.
These items aren't sold here.

Question

Is English taught here?
Are the results published?

When to use it

Three situations

Receiver is more important
We care about what is done, not who does it.

The letters are delivered every morning.

Unknown agent
We don't know who does it.

My bike is stolen regularly.

Obvious agent
It's clear who does it.

The rules are explained at the start.

By + agent

Only mention the doer when it matters

The building is cleaned by a professional team. (interesting)
These cars are made by a factory in Japan.
English is spoken all over the world. (no "by" needed)
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

English is speak all over the world.

English is spoken all over the world.

The product is maked in Brazil.

The product is made in Brazil.

The letters are deliver every morning.

The letters are delivered every morning.

The results is published online.

The results are published online.
