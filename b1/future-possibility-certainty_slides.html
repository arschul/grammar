The big picture

A scale of certainty

Possibility

May, might, could

I might still be at home next year. (possible, not sure)
You could live with a friend. (possible option)
She might not come to the party. (possibly won't happen)
Near Certainty

Probably — word order matters

I will probably get a new job next year. (after will)
I probably won't have enough money. (before won't)
She will probably be late.
Full Certainty

Definitely — same word order rule

I will definitely miss them! (after will)
He definitely won't forget your birthday. (before won't)
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She might to come tomorrow.

She might come tomorrow.

I definitely will go.

I will definitely go.

I probably won't to pass.

I probably won't pass.

She could to live with a friend.

She could live with a friend.
