The five forms

Five ways to talk about ability

can
general ability — present
could
general ability — past
be able to
general ability — more formal, more tenses
manage to
achieved despite difficulty + base verb
succeed in
achieved despite difficulty + verb‑ing
Can & Could

Present vs past ability

She can speak three languages. (present ability)
I can't swim very well. (lack of ability)
When I was six, I could already read. (past ability)
He couldn't ride a bike until he was ten.
Be Able To

Same meaning as can — more formal, more flexible.

She is able to work under pressure.
(= she can work under pressure)

I wasn't able to finish the test in time.
(useful in tenses where "can" doesn't work)

Manage To & Succeed In

A specific achievement, despite difficulty

We managed to find a taxi at midnight. (manage to + base verb)
She managed to finish the report before the deadline.
He succeeded in passing the exam after three attempts. (succeed in + ‑ing)
They succeeded in fixing the problem before the show.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She succeeded to pass the exam.

She succeeded in passing the exam.

I managed passing the test.

I managed to pass the test.

When I was young, I can swim fast.

When I was young, I could swim fast.

I am able swim very well.

I am able to swim very well.
