Should Have /
Shouldn't Have

I should have brought an umbrella.

should have + past participle
The contrast

What happened vs what should have happened

Should have
Right thing — but it didn't happen

I should have brought an umbrella. (I didn't — and it rained)

Shouldn't have
Wrong thing — but it did happen

He shouldn't have shouted. (he did — that was wrong)

Should have

The right thing — that didn't happen

I should have brought an umbrella. (didn't bring one, got wet)
We should have left earlier. (left late, missed the bus)
He should have studied for the test. (didn't, failed)
Shouldn't have

The wrong thing — that did happen

He shouldn't have shouted at his sister. (he did shout — wrong)
They shouldn't have spent all their money. (they did spend it)
I shouldn't have eaten so much pizza. (I did — now I feel sick)
💡 All three forms
Positive

You should have told me sooner.

Negative

You shouldn't have told him the secret.

Question

Should you have told her?
→ Yes, I should have.

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

You should have study harder.

You should have studied harder.

He should has left already.

He should have left already.

She shouldn't have went to the party.

She shouldn't have gone to the party.

I should have tell you yesterday.

I should have told you yesterday.
