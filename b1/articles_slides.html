Articles:
A/An, The, No Article

I saw a dog. The dog was friendly. Ø Dogs are loyal.

a / an the
The choice

Three options, one decision: how specific is it?

a / an
first mention · general singular

I saw a dog in the park.

the
specific · already known

The dog I saw yesterday came back.

Ø
general plural / uncountable

Ø Dogs are loyal animals.

A / An

Singular, not-yet-specific nouns

A veterinarian takes care of animals. (any vet, in general)
I heard there is a new veterinarian in town. (first mention)
She is an engineer. (vowel sound → an)
The

When both people know which one

The school counselor talked to us about careers. (our specific counselor)
The elephant is known for its intelligence. (elephants as a species)
Wolves sometimes howl at the moon. (only one moon)
No article (Ø)

Speaking generally

Ø Dolphins and whales are very intelligent. (plural, general)
Ø Stress can be an issue when working with animals. (uncountable, general)
Ø Music makes people happy. · Ø Water is essential for life.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I saw the dog in the park. (first mention)

I saw a dog in the park.

She plays a piano every day.

She plays the piano every day.

The dogs are loyal animals. (general)

Ø Dogs are loyal animals.

She is a honest person.

She is an honest person.
