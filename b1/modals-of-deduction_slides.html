Modals of
Deduction

This story can't be true — the details don't seem right.

modal + base verb how sure are you?
The idea

Guessing from evidence

When you're not stating a fact but making a logical guess, use a modal of deduction. The verb after it is always the base infinitive.

The structure
modal (+ not) + base infinitive

The news must be fake.
(never "must to be" / "must is")

The idea
evidence → conclusion

She isn't answering. She must be in a meeting.
(a guess, not a fact)

Rule 1

must — you're sure it's true

Use must when the evidence makes you about 99% certain something is true.

You've been working since 6 a.m. — you must be exhausted.
The lights are on. They must be back from their trip.
They wrote the book themselves, so they must know the answer.
Rule 2

might / may / could — you're not sure

Use these three when something is possible but you can't be certain. They mean the same here.

This story might be true, but it may not be 100% accurate.
The article has no sources — it could be fake news.
I'm not sure where Ana is. She may be at the gym.
Rule 3

can't — you're sure it's NOT true

The opposite of must in deduction is can't — never mustn't.

99% sure it's true → The news must be fake.
not sure (maybe) → That story might / may / could be fake.
99% sure it's NOT true → This story can't be true.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

The news must to be fake.

Correct: The news must be fake.

He mustn't be at home — the lights are off.

Correct: He can't be at home.

She must is very tired.

Correct: She must be very tired.

That story can be true — it's impossible!

Correct: That story can't be true.

✓ After a modal, use the base infinitive without "to".

She mustn't be at work — her office is dark.

She can't be at work — her office is dark.

✓ For impossible deductions, use "can't", not "mustn't".

It might raining outside.

It might be raining outside.

✓ The modal needs "be" before the -ing form.

He must knows the answer — he's the teacher.

He must know the answer — he's the teacher.

✓ Never conjugate the verb after a modal.
