B1 · English Grammar

First
Conditional

If it rains tomorrow, I will stay at home.

if + present will + base verb
The idea

A real, likely future situation

Used for specific possible situations and their probable results — not general truths (that's zero conditional).

Examples

If it rains tomorrow, I will stay at home.

If you study hard, you will pass the exam.

The structure

if + present simple

→ will + base verb

Order is flexible — comma if "if" comes first.

Word order

Either order works

If it rains, I will stay at home. (if-clause first → comma)
I will stay at home if it rains. (result first → no comma)
She will miss the bus if she doesn't hurry.
Beyond "will"

Replace will with other modals

might
uncertainty

If it's warm, we might go to the beach.

can
possibility

If you finish early, you can leave.

should
advice

If you feel ill, you should see a doctor.

In real sentences

See it in action

If he doesn't apologise, she won't forgive him.
Will you come to the party if I invite you?
What will you do if you miss the flight?
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

If it will rain, I will stay home.

If it rains, I will stay home.

If you will study hard, you will pass.

If you study hard, you will pass.

I will to call you if I'm late.

I will call you if I'm late. (no "to")

She wills miss the bus if she's late.

She will miss the bus. (will never takes -s)
