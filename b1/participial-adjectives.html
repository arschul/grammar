Participial Adjectives

-ing vs -ed adjectives

Many adjectives come in pairs: one ending in -ing and one in -ed. They come from the same verb but have different meanings.

-ing adjective → describes the thing or situation that causes the feeling
-ed adjective → describes how a person (or animal) feels
The film was boring. → The film caused boredom. (thing → -ing)
I was bored. → I felt boredom. (person → -ed)
The news was shocking. → The news caused shock. (thing → -ing)
We were shocked. → We felt shock. (person → -ed)

Common mistakes

⚠ Watch out:
✗ I was boring at the party. → ✓ I was bored at the party. (you felt boredom — use -ed)
✗ The documentary was very interested. → ✓ The documentary was very interesting. (it caused interest — use -ing)
✗ She gave an excited speech. → ✓ She gave an exciting speech. (the speech caused excitement — use -ing)
✗ It was an exhausted journey. → ✓ It was an exhausting journey. (the journey caused exhaustion — use -ing)
