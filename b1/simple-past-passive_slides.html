Simple Past
Passive

The window was broken. The letters were sent.

was / were past participle
Active vs passive

Same event, different focus

Active — focus on the doer

Someone broke the window.
My mum made the cake.

Passive — focus on what happened

The window was broken.
The cake was made by my mum.

The structure

was/were + past participle

When to use it

Three situations

Focus on what happened
The doer is less important.

The phone was found on the bus.

Unknown doer
We don't know who did it.

My bag was stolen at the station.

Unimportant doer
It doesn't matter who.

The pyramids were built thousands of years ago.

The three forms

Positive, negative and question

The cake was made by my mum. (singular → was)
The exam wasn't cancelled. The letters weren't sent on time.
Was the letter delivered?  |  Were the students told?
By + agent

Mentioning the doer (optional)

The painting was created by Da Vinci. (doer is important)
The new school was opened by the mayor.
My bag was stolen. (no "by" — doer unknown)
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

The letter was sended yesterday.

The letter was sent yesterday.

The cake were eaten by the children.

The cake was eaten by the children.

The films was enjoyed by everyone.

The films were enjoyed by everyone.

The school opened by the mayor.

The school was opened by the mayor.
