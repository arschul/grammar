B1 · English Grammar

Past
Perfect

The action that happened before another past action.

had already before by the time
The idea

What is the past perfect?

It shows that one past action happened before another. We use it to put two past events in the right order.

Examples

She had already left when I arrived.

By the time the film started, we had found our seats.

The structure

had + past participle

Marks the earlier of two past actions.

The order of events

Which tense for which event?

earlier action — had + past participle later action — simple past

When I arrived, she had already gone home.

She left first. Then I arrived.

He had never seen snow before he moved to Canada.

He moved to Canada. Before that, no snow.

They had finished dinner by the time the guests arrived.

Dinner was finished first. Guests arrived second.

💡 Handy shortcut

Already, never and just go right after had.

She had already finished when I called.

He had never tried sushi before that night.

In real sentences

The signal words that call for it

by the time

By the time we got there, the show had already started.

after

After she had read the instructions, she assembled the furniture.

no signal word

I realised I had forgotten my keys.

before

He had never tried sushi before that evening.

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

When I arrived, she already left.

When I arrived, she had already left.

He had went to bed before midnight.

He had gone to bed before midnight.

By the time she called, I had fell asleep.

By the time she called, I had fallen asleep.

She hadn't never visited Paris before that trip.

She hadn't visited Paris before that trip.

Remember this

Earlier action
had + past participle
Later action
simple past
Signal words
before · after · by the time
Watch out
gone, not went
