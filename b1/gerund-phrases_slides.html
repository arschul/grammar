Gerund
Phrases

Swimming every day is great exercise.

verb + -ing used as a noun
The idea

An -ing word acting as a noun

A gerund is the -ing form of a verb used as a noun. A gerund phrase adds its own objects or modifiers.

Examples

Swimming every day is great exercise. (as subject)

I enjoy reading in the evenings. (as object)

Two roles

[verb+ing …] + main verb

main verb + [verb+ing …]

As subject — always singular verb.

As subject

Tells us what the sentence is about

Swimming every day is great exercise.
Learning a new language takes a lot of patience.
Working from home has advantages and disadvantages.

Always a singular verb — even when the gerund phrase feels "many".

As object

After these verbs, always -ing

enjoyavoidkeep finishmindconsider suggestpractiserecommend dislikeimaginemiss can't stand
I enjoy reading in the evenings.
She avoids eating fast food.
After prepositions

A verb after a preposition is always -ing

She is good at solving problems.
He is interested in learning photography.
I'm tired of waiting.
We talked about moving to a bigger flat.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

Swim every day is good for you.

Swimming every day is good for you.

I enjoy to read in the evenings.

I enjoy reading in the evenings.

She is good at solve problems.

She is good at solving problems.

He finished to paint the house.

He finished painting the house.
