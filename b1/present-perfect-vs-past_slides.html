Present Perfect
vs Simple Past

I have been to Japan.  ·  I went to Japan last year.

time unknown specific time
The key question

Does the time matter?

Present Perfect
Time unknown / not important

I have been to Japan.
(some time in my life — when doesn't matter)

Simple Past
Specific time mentioned or understood

I went to Japan last year.
(specific time → simple past)

Present perfect

When to use it

I have been to Japan. (life experience — when = not important)
She has tried sushi before. (at some point)
Have you ever been to London? (ever = any time in your life)
Simple past

When to use it

I went to Japan last year. (specific time)
She tried sushi when she was in Tokyo. (specific situation)
They saw that film on Saturday. (specific day)
Signal words

The word often tells you which tense to use.

Present Perfect
ever · never · before · once · twice
in my life · this week / year · recently
Simple Past
yesterday · last week · last year
in 2019 · two days ago · when I was a child
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I have went to Paris.

I have gone to Paris.

Did you ever eat Thai food?

Have you ever eaten Thai food?

She has called me yesterday.

She called me yesterday.

I have gone there last year.

I went there last year.
