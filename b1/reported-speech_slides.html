The idea

Reporting what someone said

Direct → Reported

"I am tired," she said. → She said (that) she was tired.

"I work here." → He said he worked there.

The rule

Tenses shift one step back into the past.

"that" after the verb is optional.

Say vs Tell

The most important difference

say
No person object directly after

She said she was hungry. ✓
She said to me she was hungry. ✓
She said me she was hungry. ✗

tell
Always needs a person object

She told me she was hungry. ✓
She told that she was hungry. ✗

Other changes

Pronouns and time expressions

"I left my bag here yesterday."
→ She said she had left her bag there the day before.
today → that day  |  tomorrow → the next day  |  now → then
here → there  |  this → that  |  I / my → she / her
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She said me she was tired.

She told me she was tired. (or: she said she was tired)

He said that he will come.

He said that he would come.

She told that the film was good.

She said the film was good. (or: told me)

He said he can do it.

He said he could do it.

She told me that she was late.

He said he will come tomorrow.

He said he would come the next day.

She told that the food was amazing.

She said the food was amazing.

✓ tell needs a person object

He said me that he can swim.

He told me he could swim.
