Present Perfect
Progressive

I have been studying English for three years.

have/has been verb + -ing
The structure

have / has + been + verb‑ing

Positive

I have been studying all day.
She has been working here since 2020.

Negative

They haven't been sleeping well.
He hasn't been eating properly.

Question

Have you been waiting long?
Has she been crying?

Use 1 — still in progress

Started in the past, still happening

I have been studying English for three years. (still happening)
She has been working here since 2020.
They haven't been sleeping well lately.
Use 2 — recent visible result

A continuous action explains now

Your eyes are red. Have you been crying? (eyes explain it)
I'm exhausted — I have been running.
The ground is wet. It has been raining.
💡 Key contrasts
Present Perfect

I have read three books this month. (completed — how many)

Present Perfect Progressive

I have been reading all afternoon. (ongoing — how long)

for + duration (three years)  |  since + starting point (2020)

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I have been knowing her for years.

I have known her for years. (stative verb)

She has been working here since two years.

She has been working here for two years.

I have working here since January.

I have been working here since January.

How long have you been wait?

How long have you been waiting?
