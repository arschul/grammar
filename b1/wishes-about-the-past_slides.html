Wishes About
the Past

I wish I had studied harder.

wish + had past participle
The idea

Regrets — the past can't be changed

Wish + had + p.p.
Right thing — didn't happen

I wish I had brought a jacket.
(I didn't bring one — cold)

Wish + hadn't + p.p.
Wrong thing — did happen

She wishes she hadn't quit her job.
(she quit — now regrets it)

Wish vs reality

Every wish is the opposite of what happened

I wish I had brought a jacket.
→ I didn't bring one and got cold.
She wishes she hadn't quit her job.
→ She quit — now she regrets it.
We wish we had left earlier.
→ We left late and missed something.
Both forms

Wish + had / hadn't + past participle

I wish I had asked for help. (I didn't ask → regret)
I wish I hadn't said that. (I did say it → regret)
He wishes he had saved more money. (he/she → wishes)
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I wish I would have studied harder.

I wish I had studied harder.

She wish she hadn't said that.

She wishes she hadn't said that.

I wish I have asked for directions.

I wish I had asked for directions.

We wish we would have left earlier.

We wish we had left earlier.

Recap

Remember this

Didn't happen → regret
wish + had + p.p.
Did happen → regret
wish + hadn't + p.p.
he/she/it
wishes (not wish)
Never
"would have" in this pattern

Always "had" — never "would have" or "have" in a past wish.

Compare with should have

Wish vs should have

I wish I had studied more. (personal regret)
I should have studied more. (criticism/regret — same meaning)
Both express regret about the past — both use past perfect structure.
