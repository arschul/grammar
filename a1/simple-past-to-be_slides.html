Simple Past:
To Be

Yesterday, it was cold. We were at home.

was were
The choice

Was or were?

I / he / she / it
was
I was very tired after the journey.
you / we / they
were
We were at the beach all day.
More examples

She was a teacher for twenty years.

They were late for the meeting.

The forms

Positive, negative and question

Positive

I was hungry.
They were happy.

Negative

I wasn't hungry.
They weren't happy.

Question

Was she at home?
Were they ready?

Questions can start with a question word: Where was he? · How long were they in London?

💡 Good to know

Only two contractions to learn.

wasn't

I / he / she / it was not

weren't

you / we / they were not

In real sentences

See it in action

I wasn't hungry, so I went to bed early.
The film was really boring but the actors were great.
Were you at the party last night?
The instructions weren't very clear.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

They was very happy.

They were very happy.

Was you at school?

Were you at school?

It were a great concert.

It was a great concert.

She not was at home.

She wasn't at home.
