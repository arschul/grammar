What is happening right now?

am / is / are verb + -ing
The idea

What is the present continuous?

We use it to talk about things happening right now, at this exact moment. It always needs am / is / are + verb‑ing.

Examples

I am working right now.

She is reading a book.

They are playing football.

The structure

subject + am/is/are + verb‑ing

Both parts are required — never drop am/is/are.

The choice

Am, is or are?

I
am
I am eating breakfast. (I'm)
he / she / it
is
He is sleeping. (He's)
you / we / they
are
We are studying English. (We're)

Negative: isn't / aren't — She isn't reading. · We aren't watching TV.

Spelling

How do I make the -ing form?

Most verbs → add -ing

play → playing
read → reading
wait → waiting

Silent -e → drop e, add -ing

write → writing
make → making
dance → dancing

Short verb + vowel + consonant → double consonant

sit → sitting
run → running
swim → swimming

In real sentences

See it in action

Look! The dog is chasing the cat in the garden.
Right now, my sister is doing her homework upstairs.
Can you be quiet? I am trying to study for my test.
Listen! Someone is knocking on the door.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

I cooking dinner.

I am cooking dinner.

The dogs is barking.

The dogs are barking.

She is writeing a letter.

She is writing a letter.

She not is reading.

She isn't reading.
