A1 · English Grammar

Simple Present:
Do / Does

Do you work here? She doesn't live here.

do / don't does / doesn't
The structure

How does it work?

Positive: I/you/we/they use the base verb. He/she/it adds -s / -es.
Negative and question: use do / does — main verb always stays base form.

Examples

I work in a hospital. / She works in a school.

She doesn't work on Sundays. / Does he play football?

The rule

do → I / you / we / they

does → he / she / it

Main verb = base form after do/does.

The forms

Positive, negative and question

Positive

I work here.
She works here.

Negative

I don't work here.
She doesn't work here.

Question

Do you work here?
Does she work here?

Questions can start with a question word: Where do they live? · What does she do?

Spelling

He / she / it: when to add -es?

Most verbs → add -s

work → works
live → lives
play → plays

ch / sh / x / o / s → add -es

watch → watches
go → goes
fix → fixes

consonant + y → change to -ies

study → studies
try → tries
fly → flies

In real sentences

See it in action

She studies English every day and watches TV in the evening.
They don't speak French — they speak Portuguese.
Does he play football at the weekend?
Where do they live? — They live in London.
⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

She don't like it.

She doesn't like it.

Does he works here?

Does he work here?

He don't speaks English.

He doesn't speak English.

Do she live near here?

Does she live near here?
