B2 · English Grammar

Mixed
Conditionals

If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now.

two time frames past & present
The idea

Two times, one sentence

Standard conditionals stay in one time. A mixed conditional links an unreal moment in one time to a result in another.

Examples

If I had saved more, I would own a house now.

If he weren't shy, he would have spoken up.

The two patterns

past → present

present → past

The if-clause and the result clause point at different times.

Type A · past → present

Unreal past, unreal present

If + past perfect, ... would + base verb (now)

If I had saved more money, I would own a flat now.
If she hadn't missed the flight, she would be here.
If they had taken the medicine, they wouldn't feel ill today.
Type B · present → past

Unreal present, unreal past

If + past simple, ... would have + past participle (then)

If he weren't so careless, he wouldn't have lost his keys.
If I spoke Spanish, I would have applied for the job.
If she were a planner, she would have finished on time.
Don't confuse them

Third vs. mixed

3rd (all past):  If I had studied, I would have passed.
Mixed A:  If I had studied, I would be a doctor now.
Mixed B:  If I were smarter, I would have passed then.

Ask: when is the condition? When is the result?

⚠ Watch out

Common mistakes

If I would have known, I would be there now.

Correct: If I had known, I would be there now.

If she were lucky, she would went by ambulance.

Correct: she would have gone by ambulance.

If he had trained, he will be fit now.

Correct: he would be fit now.

If they weren't lazy, they would have finish it.

Correct: they would have finished it.

Spot the mistake

If she had studied harder, she will be a doctor now.

... she would be a doctor now.

✓ Present result needs 'would', not 'will'.

If I were you, I wouldn't said that yesterday.

... I wouldn't have said that yesterday.

✓ Past result needs 'would have' + past participle.

If he hadn't lied, we would trusted him now.

... we would trust him now.

✓ Present result: 'would' + base verb.

If I would have known, I would be there now.

If I had known, I would be there now.

✓ No 'would have' in the if-clause — use the past perfect.
