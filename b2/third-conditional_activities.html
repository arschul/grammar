Third Conditional

What is the Third Conditional?

The third conditional refers to imaginary situations in the past. We use it to speculate about things that did not happen, and to imagine a different outcome. It often expresses regret or criticism.

IF clause: if + past perfect (had + past participle)
Main clause: would have / could have / might have + past participle

Examples

If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam. (I didn't study, so I failed)
She would have got the job if she had arrived on time. (she was late)
If he had saved more money, he could have bought a car. (he didn't save)
We wouldn't have got lost if we had used the map. (we didn't use it)

Contractions in Speech

In spoken English, had and would have are often contracted.

If I'd known, I would've told you. (= If I had known, I would have told you.)
She'd have come if she'd been invited.
⚠ Common mistake: Do NOT use would have in the if-clause.
✗ If I would have known, I would have called.
✓ If I had known, I would have called.
