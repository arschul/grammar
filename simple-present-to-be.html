Simple Present: To Be

The Verb To Be in the Simple Present

The verb to be has three forms in the present simple: am, is, and are. It is used to describe people, things, feelings, nationalities, jobs, and more.

I → am  |  He / She / It → is  |  You / We / They → are

Positive Sentences

I am a student.
She is from Brazil.
They are very friendly.
It is cold today.
We are in the classroom.

Negative Sentences

Add not after the verb. Common contractions: isn't, aren't. Note: am not has no standard contraction.

I am not tired.
He isn't (is not) a doctor.
We aren't (are not) late.

Questions

Move the verb to be before the subject to form a question.

Are you ready?
Is she your teacher?
Am I late?
Where are they from?
⚠ Common mistakes:
✗ She is a doctor? (question without inversion) → ✓ Is she a doctor?
✗ They is happy. → ✓ They are happy.
✗ He are my friend. → ✓ He is my friend.
